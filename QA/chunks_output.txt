Chunk 1:
Introduction to Artificial Intelligence 
Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that 
are programmed to think like humans and mimic their actions. The term may also be 
applied to any machine that exhibits traits associated with a human mind such as learning 
and problem-solving.

Chunk 2:
Types of Artificial Intelligence  
Narrow AI: Designed to perform a specific task such as virtual assistants, recommendation 
systems, and facial recognition.  
General AI: Systems that possess the ability to perform any intellectual task that a human 
can do. This type of AI does not yet exist.

Chunk 3:
Super AI: Surpasses human intelligence and can perform tasks better than humans in all 
aspects. This is mostly theoretical.  
 
Applications of AI  
Healthcare: Disease diagnosis, medical imaging, drug discovery.  
Finance: Fraud detection, algorithmic trading, credit scoring.  
Education: Personalized learning, automated grading.

Chunk 4:
Transportation: Self-driving cars, traffic prediction.  
 
Machine Learning and Deep Learning  
Machine Learning is a subset of AI that focuses on systems that learn from data. Deep 
Learning is a subset of ML that uses neural networks with many layers.  
 
Challenges in AI  
Data privacy, algorithmic bias, computational cost, ethical concerns.

Chunk 5:
Conclusion  
Artificial Intelligence is transforming industries and will continue to shape the future of 
technology and society.
